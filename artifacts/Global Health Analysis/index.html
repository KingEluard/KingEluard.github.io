Global Health Analysis

Time Series and Logistic Regression Analysis of Global Health, Environmental, and Socioeconomic Data (2012-2021)

Project Timeline: 2024
Academic Project - Langara College
Tools: R, RStudio, Statistical Analysis

Project Overview

This comprehensive analysis examines global health patterns and their relationships with environmental and socioeconomic factors across a 9-year period (2012-2021). Using advanced statistical methods in R, we conducted time series analysis and logistic regression to uncover significant regional disparities and temporal trends in global health indicators.

Key Objectives

  • Analyze temporal trends in global health indicators from 2012-2021
  • Identify regional disparities in health outcomes across different countries
  • Explore relationships between health, environmental, and socioeconomic variables
  • Apply time series and logistic regression techniques for predictive modeling
  • Create compelling data storytelling to communicate findings

Key Findings & Insights

Regional Health Disparities

Significant variations in health outcomes were identified across different geographic regions, with Sub-Saharan Africa and parts of South Asia showing persistently lower health indicator scores compared to developed regions.

Temporal Trends

Overall positive trends in global health indicators over the 9-year period, with accelerated improvements in maternal and child health outcomes in developing regions.

Environmental Correlations

Strong correlations discovered between environmental factors (air quality, water access) and population health outcomes, particularly in urban versus rural areas.

Socioeconomic Impact

GDP per capita and education levels emerged as significant predictors of health outcomes, with threshold effects identified for maximum health impact.
